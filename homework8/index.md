# **Comparative Analysis: Bernoulli Process, Law of Large Numbers, and Random Walk Convergence**

## **Introduction**

This essay discusses the conceptual and mathematical analogies between two previous simulations:

1. **Homework 4**, which modeled the **Law of Large Numbers** (LLN) through repeated Bernoulli trials and the
stabilization of the relative frequency of successes.

2. **Homework 7**, which modeled a **random walk** generated by repeated independent security events (breach/no-breach)
evolving according to a **Binomial distribution**.

Despite their different interpretations one in probability theory and one inspired by cybersecurity risk both
simulations are fundamentally driven by **Bernoulli processes** and illustrate convergence laws that emerge from
independent random events.

### **2. Pascal‚Äôs Triangle and Combinatorial Coefficients**

The recursive rule of **Pascal‚Äôs triangle**

<div class="formula-box">
    (<span style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:middle;">
        <sup style="display:block; line-height:1; font-size:0.7em;">n</sup>
        <sub style="display:block; line-height:1; font-size:0.7em;">k</sub>
    </span>)
    =
    (<span style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:middle;">
        <sup style="display:block; line-height:1; font-size:0.7em;">n-1</sup>
        <sub style="display:block; line-height:1; font-size:0.7em;">k-1</sub>
    </span>)
    +
    (<span style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:middle;">
        <sup style="display:block; line-height:1; font-size:0.7em;">n-1</sup>
        <sub style="display:block; line-height:1; font-size:0.7em;">k</sub>
    </span>)
</div>

underlies both simulations.<br><br>
Each new layer of the triangle mirrors how outcomes expand as new independent trials are added:

- In **Homework 4**, adding a trial updates f(t) through f<sub>t</sub> = f<sub>t-1</sub> + (X<sub>t</sub> -
f<sub>t-1</sub>)/t an **online** version of Pascal‚Äôs recursive growth.

- In **Homework 7**, each additional week expands the reachable scores S<sub>t</sub> = S<sub>t-1</sub> ¬± 1 geometrically
equivalent to moving along Pascal‚Äôs triangle diagonals.

Thus, the numerical progression of coefficients directly parallels the evolution of possible cumulative states.

### **4. Relation with the Fibonacci Sequence**

Although Fibonacci numbers arise from a different recurrence F<sub>n</sub> = F<sub>n-1</sub> + F<sub>n-2</sub>, they
share the same **combinatorial logic** that defines Pascal‚Äôs triangle. If one traces the diagonals of Pascal‚Äôs triangle,
the sums along shallow diagonals produce Fibonacci numbers. This highlights a deeper connection: both Pascal‚Äôs triangle
and random-walk counting depend on recursive additive structures that generate exponential or polynomial growth from
simple base cases.

## **Theoretical Relationships Illustrated**

1. **Bernoulli Process ‚Üí Binomial Distribution:**  
   Both start from i.i.d. Bernoulli trials whose sum is Binomial(n,p).

2. **Binomial Coefficients ‚Üí Pascal‚Äôs Triangle:**  
   The coefficients define transition counts between adjacent levels.

3. **Binomial Expansion ‚Üí Polynomial Growth:**  
   (p + (1-p))<sup>n</sup> = 1 ensures total probability normalization.

4. **Pascal‚Äôs Triangle ‚Üí Fibonacci Connection:**  
   Diagonal summations reveal Fibonacci recurrence inside the same combinatorial lattice.

5. **Combinatorial Coefficients ‚Üí Random-Walk Enumeration:**  
   The number of paths reaching position s = n ‚àí 2k equals (<span style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:middle;">
        <sup style="display:block; line-height:1; font-size:0.7em;">n</sup>
        <sub style="display:block; line-height:1; font-size:0.7em;">k</sub>
    </span>).

Thus, all structures from Pascal‚Äôs triangle to the random-walk histogram emerge from a **shared combinatorial foundation**.

## **Conclusion**

The Bernoulli-based Law-of-Large-Numbers simulation (Homework 4) and the Random-Walk-Binomial simulation (Homework 7) are two facets of the same mathematical truth:

Where the first emphasizes the stabilization of averages, the second generalizes to the stabilization of entire distributions visual proof that from discrete uncertainty, **statistical regularity and symmetry inevitably emerge**.
